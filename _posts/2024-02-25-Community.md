# A place to belong

## Friend group

There's something that my ex told me a long time ago.

She was talking about how she had to take part in a friend group, how she had to join in on conversations and pay attention to what people were saying even if she didn't really understand them, or she didn't care about what they said.

And back then, I asked her why. 

Why do you need to listen to people talk about stuff in their own lives, which YOU don't 
care about?

And she told me it's because you always need a "friend group". 

And that that was something that she couldn't bear to lose.

This wasn't the last I'd hear her talk about this either. She would talk about how she needed to 
fit in and join a friend group once she went to college.

She would tell me about how she was searching for friend groups, and couldn't decide which one to join. Personally, I thought it was a bit unsettling how she was comparing these groups of people and in a sense dehumanizing them. I thought it was immoral at the time, and even now, I still think it's bad to do something like that.

But I understand. It's probably the best she could do, and I doubt she fully understood the consequences of what she was doing either. 

She wasn't really as thoughtful or smart as I had given her credit for.

But she did have a point, which I've now come to deeply understand.

## A community of people 

A "friend group" or as I'd like to think of it, a "community" of people that provide value to you and who you in turn would provide value to you

I feel more and more that I want something like this.

This desire has come to me in many forms.

- Wanting a group of people that admire you
- Wanting an organization that you can work with and become an important part of
- Wanting a large group of like-minded comrades working towards the same goal
- Wanting a brotherhood of people
- Wanting a found family

I've desperately gone and tried searching for people to fulfill this desire.

People that I can talk to and hang out with, and just chill with in general.

I've had trouble relying on people, and I've never really solved this problem. When I encounter trouble or suffering, it's often because I deserve it, and when I ask other people for help with a problem like that, I can't avoid facing the fact that I'm basically taking advantage of other people to avoid the consequences of my actions.

It feels too much like I'm just using people.

I can't stop myself from seeing people as "other"

A different being or existence which is separate from me. Something that I can play with and have fun with. Something that I can test and understand and experiment with.

Beings that fill me with curiosity and makes me want to push them to their limits just to see where those limits lie.

I'm sure that deep down I'm the same, but sometimes it really doesn't feel that way.

Other people just feel like they think too differently from me, in a way that I can't understand, and when I assume that they think just like I do, I end up with misunderstandings and problems.

(Well, people like Aya and Updogg and maybe even Thanshu don't think exactly like me, but seem to react and behave in a similar fashion to me. Perhaps this is because I've changed. Perhaps this is simply a result of me getting lucky and finding people who have similar minds.)

I don't know what to do exactly to solve this problem. 

I believe the solution is two-fold. As mentioned in the brackets earlier, I will continue to search for people like me and hope to get lucky, as well as continue trying to adapt, understand and change myself in a way that helps me understand people. 

When I put it that way, it sounds fairly simple and straightforward. Perhaps because it is. Aya did it.
I love Aya so much, just for who she is and what she's been able to achieve while staying who she is. It truly is amazing.
