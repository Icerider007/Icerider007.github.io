So I took inspiration from Aya. Aya has tons of admirers and people who like her, people who say nice things to her, usually people who don't actually have a visible impact on her life.

But that's okay. It's not like it's her fault that they like her. It's not her responsibility, or her obligation to be nice to them, respond to them, or even deal with them. It's not humanly possible. It's not feasible and it's absurd to expect that kind of thing from anyone at all.

But of course, people will expect it.

What was I talking about again? Right, MYSELF.

So, if I think someone loves me, or likes me even, what I usually do is go along with them. Which is an incredibly naive and common solution for first-timers, people who haven't had a lot of people like them at all.

I remember that my ex did this, I remember that Aya did this, and I know a bunch of guys that did this, and for Aya and those guys it usually was pretty empty but there was sex so eh.

With my ex, I don't think they'd ever gotten to that point, so I dunno.

But yeah, that is a BAD solution. Because it's a goddamn pain, especially when you really don't want to be with that person. When you're not attracted to them, or when you don't want to deal with the responsibilities of a full-on relationship where you have to do your best.

So, what do I do when someone likes me? 
I thought about it. I'll acknowledge it, and I'll just kind of play around with that fact.
